Literature

This page lists work that you may find generally useful in your study of information behavior theory. This list will change and grow as we proceed through the semester.

Information Behavior Theory Books

This is a list of books on information behavior that you should be aware of and may want to own.

  • Spink, A., & Cole, C. (Eds.). (2006). New Directions in Human Information Behavior: Dordrecht, Netherlands: Springer Netherlands. http://doi.org/10.1007/1-4020-3670-1
  • Sonnenwald, D. H. (Ed.). (2016). Theory Development in the Information Sciences. University of Texas Press. Retrieved from https://books.google.com/books?id=_5xBCwAAQBAJ
  • Fidel, R. (2012). Human Information Interaction: An Ecological Approach to Information Behavior. MIT Press. Retrieved from https://books.google.com/books?id=-erlCTVFDUQC
  • Fisher, K. E., Erdelez, S., & McKechnie, L. (Eds.). (2005). Theories of Information Behavior. American Society for Information Science and Technology. Retrieved from https://books.google.com/books?id=ll6qzqhIj8wC
  • Case, D. O. (2007). Looking for information: A survey of research on information seeking, needs and behavior (2nd ed.). London: Academic Press. Retrieved from https://books.google.com/books?id=XYX_RV7Wy9QC
