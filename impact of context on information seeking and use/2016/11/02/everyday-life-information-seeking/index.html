Everyday life information seeking

Wednesday, 2 November 2016

  • Topic selection DUE (intermediate deliverable for Analysis of scholarly communication)

Assigned reading

  • Savolainen, R. (1995). Everyday life information seeking: Approaching information seeking in the context of “way of life.” Library and Information Science Research, 17(3), 259–294. http://doi.org/10.1016/0740-8188(95)90048-9

    This is in today’s list for reference only. You do not have to read it, but I may refer to it occasionally. This is the seminal article on everyday life information seeking, which is not an important area of information behavior research. One question for us is which types of everyday life information needs are we likely to address via currently available institutions or systems. Also, if you are interested in Bourdieu and Habitus, then you might want to skim through this.
  • Agosto, D. E., & Hughes-Hassell, S. (2006). Toward a model of the everyday life information needs of urban teenagers, part 1: Theoretical model. Journal of the American Society for Information Science, 57(10), 1394–1403. http://doi.org/10.1002/asi.20451

    Choose to read either this or part 2, which outlines the empirical model.
  • Agosto, D. E., & Hughes-Hassell, S. (2006). Toward a model of the everyday life information needs of urban teenagers, Part 2: Empirical model. Journal of the American Society for Information Science, 57(11), 1418–1426. http://doi.org/10.1002/asi.20452

    Choose to read either this or part 1, which outlines the theoretical model.
  • Sin, S.-C. J., & Kim, K.-S. (2013). International students’ everyday life information seeking: The informational value of social networking sites. Library and Information Science Research, 35(2), 107–116. http://doi.org/10.1016/j.lisr.2012.11.006

    Read either this or Sin and Kim (2014). These are studies produced by the same research program.
  • Sin, S.-C. J., & Kim, K.-S. (2014). Impacts of social media usage on the outcomes of students.’ Proceedings of the American Society for Information Science And Technology, 51(1), 1–4. http://doi.org/10.1002/meet.2014.14505101119

    Read either this or Sin and Kim (2013). These are studies produced by the same research program.
  • Markwei, E., & Rasmussen, E. (2015). Everyday Life Information-Seeking Behavior of Marginalized Youth: A Qualitative Study of Urban Homeless Youth in Ghana. International Information and Library Review, 47(1-2), 11–29. http://doi.org/10.1080/10572317.2015.1039425
