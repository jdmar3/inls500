Studying/analyzing information needs

Wednesday, 21 September 2016

  • Context and population DUE (intermediate deliverable for System/Service proposal)

Assigned reading

  • Herman, E. (2004). Research in progress: some preliminary and key insights into the information needs of the contemporary academic researcher. Part 1. Aslib Proceedings, 56(1), 34–47. http://doi.org/10.1108/00012530410516859

    Based on interviews with faculty at the University of Haifa, Herman investigated 11 aspects of information needs: subject, function, nature, intellectual level, viewpoint, quantity, quality/authority, date/currency, speed of delivery, place of publication/origina, and processing/packaging. This paper focuses only on the subject and function of their information needs; Part 2, listed in our optional readings, provides more detail on other aspects of their information needs.

  • Watson, B. R., & Cavanah, S. (2015). Community Information Needs: A Theory and Methodological Framework. Mass Communication and Society, 18(5), 651–673. http://doi.org/10.1080/15205436.2015.1059948

    This article considers and applies community-based participatory research as a framework within which we can better understand the information needs of groups in their context.

Today we will have a broad-based discussion about the foci of information needs research and discuss more specifically how it is conducted. This includes considering what the target outcomes of the research are. We will also discuss the underlying motivations of research in this area more specifically than in previous sessions.
